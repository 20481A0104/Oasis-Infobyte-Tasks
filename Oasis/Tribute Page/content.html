Che Guevara

Better to Die standing than to live on your knees

Che Guevara, whose full name was Ernesto "Che" Guevara, was a prominent Marxist revolutionary, guerrilla leader, and a key figure in the Cuban Revolution.

Che Guevara becomes a symbol of revolution and an iconic figure around the world. His image is famously captured in the photograph "Guerrillero Heroico" taken by Alberto Korda.

Guevara's ideas and beliefs continue to inspire leftist movements and revolutionary activists globally. His name and image are often associated with counterculture and anti-establishment movements.

Timeline Highlighting key events about his life:

  • June 14, 1928 : Ernesto Guevara de la Serna is born in Rosario, Argentina, to a middle-class family.
  • 1953 : Guevara travels across South America with his friend Alberto Granado. This journey exposes him to the social and economic inequalities prevalent in the region, which influences his political beliefs
  • 1955 : Guevara meets Fidel Castro in Mexico and becomes involved with Castro's revolutionary movement aimed at overthrowing the Cuban dictator Fulgencio Batista.
  • 1956 : Guevara, along with Fidel Castro and 80 other revolutionaries, sets sail from Mexico on the yacht Granma to initiate the Cuban Revolution.
  • 1957-1958 : Guevara plays a crucial role as a military strategist and leader in the revolutionary guerrilla warfare against Batista's forces.
  • 1959 : After the success of the Cuban Revolution, Fidel Castro's forces seize control of Havana, and Batista flees the country. Guevara becomes a prominent figure in the new Cuban government.
  • 1961 : Che Guevara becomes the Minister of Industry in Cuba and plays a significant role in the country's economic development.
  • 1965 : Guevara leaves Cuba to support other revolutionary movements around the world. He travels to Congo-Kinshasa (now the Democratic Republic of Congo) to assist rebels in their fight against the government, but the mission is unsuccessful.
  • 1966 : Guevara re-emerges in Bolivia to support the National Liberation Army (ELN) in their fight against the government. However, due to lack of local support and the Bolivian military's efforts, Guevara is captured by the Bolivian army.
  • October 9, 1967 : Che Guevara is executed by the Bolivian army in the village of La Higuera, Bolivia.
